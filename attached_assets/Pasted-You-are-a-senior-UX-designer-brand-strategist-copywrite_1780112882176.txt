You are a senior UX designer, brand strategist, copywriter, and front-end engineer.

I need you to update the **Grand Strand Ally** homepage so it feels:
- less aggressive
- more corporate
- more calm and trustworthy
- more advisory and professional
- more likely to inspire a business owner or office manager to call

## Core objective
Keep the site visually aligned with grandstrandautomation.com and keep Grand Strand Ally’s positioning around:
- cost clarity
- overlapping tools
- Microsoft 365 support
- compliance-minded information technology support
- month-to-month service
- practical process improvement

But soften the homepage tone so it feels more like:
- a trusted advisor
- a business consultant
- a reliable local technology partner
- a company that helps bring clarity and structure

It should feel less like:
- a hard-hitting cost-savings pitch
- a fear-based sales page
- an aggressive audit page
- a page that assumes the visitor has done something wrong

## Tone shift requirements
Rewrite the homepage copy to sound:
- more measured
- more polished
- more executive-friendly
- more reassuring
- more corporate but still approachable
- more confidence-building
- less confrontational
- less urgent in a pushy way

### Important tone rules
Avoid overly aggressive phrases such as:
- stop overpaying
- wasting money
- duplicate tools you do not need
- surprise invoices
- weak processes
- gaps everywhere
- messy environments
- too many vendors and nobody owns anything
- strong “problem first” language unless softened

Prefer calmer, corporate language such as:
- improve visibility into current information technology spend
- identify opportunities to simplify and consolidate
- gain a clearer view of vendor and tool usage
- support better operational and compliance outcomes
- create a cleaner and more manageable environment
- strengthen controls and reduce unnecessary complexity
- improve consistency across support and user lifecycle processes

## Homepage rewrite goals
The homepage should:
- still convert
- still mention cost analysis
- still highlight potential cost savings
- still talk about overlapping tools
- still include compliance throughout
- still support calls and form submissions

But it should do so with a more professional, boardroom-safe tone.

## Visual and UX goals
Do NOT radically redesign the site.
Keep the existing layout style and visual family aligned with grandstrandautomation.com:
- same header style
- same section rhythm
- same clean card structure
- same navy / off-white palette
- same restrained premium feel

But improve the homepage by:
- reducing visual and verbal intensity
- increasing white space where helpful
- making section intros shorter and more composed
- making the hero feel more elevated and less salesy
- making the page easier to scan
- improving trust and readability

## Critical writing rules
All visible site copy must use fully spelled-out terms.

Use:
- Microsoft 365
- small and medium businesses
- information technology
- multi-factor authentication
- onboarding and offboarding

Do NOT use:
- M365
- SMB
- unexplained acronyms

## Primary messaging strategy
Shift the homepage from:
“you are overspending and have problems”

Toward:
“we help businesses gain clarity, simplify support, improve oversight, and identify practical opportunities to reduce cost and strengthen compliance-minded operations.”

## Homepage structure to keep
Keep the homepage structure roughly like this:
1. Sticky header
2. Hero
3. Intro / why businesses reach out
4. 3 calm issue/opportunity cards
5. 3 core systems or service pillars
6. Who we help
7. How we work
8. Why Grand Strand Ally
9. Cost analysis / what we review
10. Frequently asked questions
11. Final call to action
12. Footer

## Hero section rewrite requirements
The hero is the most important tone change.

### Hero should feel:
- composed
- confident
- clear
- advisory
- professional

### Hero should NOT feel:
- alarming
- accusatory
- overly aggressive
- too focused on “you are wasting money”

### Hero message direction
The hero should communicate that Grand Strand Ally helps businesses:
- understand their current information technology environment
- improve visibility into current costs and vendor overlap
- simplify support and Microsoft 365 administration
- strengthen compliance-minded processes
- identify sensible next steps without unnecessary complexity

### Hero copy guidelines
Use:
- one strong but calm headline
- one short, polished supporting paragraph
- a trust row
- one or two call-to-action buttons

### CTA tone guidance
I still want to support conversion, but the homepage should feel more corporate and call-friendly.

Use these call-to-action options:
Primary:
- Book a Free Cost Analysis
or
- Schedule a Free Cost Analysis

Secondary:
- See Services
or
- Talk With Us

If needed, slightly soften the primary button label sitewide from “Book” to “Schedule” if it improves tone consistency.

- Compliance-minded support
- Month-to-month flexibility
